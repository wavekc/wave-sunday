Great People Who Go Beyond Self-Love

Ephesians 5:22–33  ·  Eph. 5:28

We usually focus on this verse from Eph. 5:22–33…

22 Wives, submit to your own husbands, as to the Lord. 23 For the husband is head of the wife, as also Christ is head of the church; and He is the Savior of the body.

Eph. 5:22–23

Paul expounds on husband's love for his wife from v. 25–33

25 Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ also loved the church and gave Himself for her.

Eph. 5:25

= Self-giving love, covenantal love
≠ Self-centered love, transactional love

Love not merely as an emotion, but as responsibility and care — This may not be easily understood at first glance

28 So husbands ought to love their own wives as their own bodies; he who loves his wife loves himself.

Eph. 5:28

How do you take care of your body? What do you do when your skin is wounded?

26 that He might sanctify and cleanse her with the washing of water by the word, 27 that He might present her to Himself a glorious church, not having spot or wrinkle or any such thing, but that she should be holy and without blemish.

Eph. 5:26–27

Paul doesn't deny the existence of self-love — Oneness comes when we take care of other as ourselves

29 For no one ever hated his own flesh, but nourishes and cherishes it, just as the Lord does the church. 30 For we are members of His body, … 31 "For this reason a man shall leave his father and mother and be joined to his wife, and the two shall become one flesh (Gen. 2:24)."

Eph. 5:29–31

Paul desires this reality to be reflected in Christian families, and he emphasizes it once again

33 Nevertheless let each one of you in particular so love his own wife as himself, and let the wife see that she respects her husband.

Eph. 5:33

Men are called to show toward their wives the same love that Christ has shown toward us by loving their wives as they love themselves

It takes God to love like this!
