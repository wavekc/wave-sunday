Sunday Worship · 2025.05.03 Jesus Who Knows Us

Jesus Who Knows Us

John 1:27–51  ·  Wave Church Sunday Worship

John the Baptist's Testimony — Jn. 1:27, 34

27It is He who, coming after me, is preferred before me, whose sandal strap I am not worthy to loose  … 34And I have seen and testified that this is the Son of God. (Jn 1:27, 34)

Event described after Jesus' 40-day fast: John the Baptist's testimony of Jesus
John the Baptist is filled with humility
At Bethany Beyond the Jordan — Jn. 1:28, 35–37

28These things were done in Bethabara beyond the Jordan, where John was baptizing.  … 35Again, the next day, John stood with two of his disciples. 36And looking at Jesus as He walked, he said, "Behold the Lamb of God!" 37The two disciples heard him speak, and they followed Jesus. (Jn. 1:28, 35–37)

Jesus was in a place called Bethany (Bethabara) — One of them was Andrew, Peter's brother
"What do you seek?" — Jn. 1:38–40

38Then Jesus turned, and seeing them following, said to them, "What do you seek?" They said to Him, "Rabbi" (which is to say, when translated, Teacher), "where are You staying?" 39He said to them, "Come and see." … (Jn. 1:38–40)

Those who wanted to become a rabbi's disciples would watch that rabbi's life closely, walk with him, eat with him — They must be covered by Rabbi's dust!

Andrew doesn't become His disciple this time. There's official calling later (Mt. 4:18–22)
The Tenth Hour — Jn. 1:38–40

39…They came and saw where He was staying, and remained with Him that day (now it was about the tenth hour). 40One of the two who heard John speak, and followed Him, was Andrew, Simon Peter's brother. (Jn. 1:38–40)

In Jewish time, the daytime begins around 6AM
Simon Becomes Cephas — Jn. 1:41–42

41He first found his own brother Simon, and said to him, "We have found the Messiah" (which is translated, the Christ). 42And he brought him to Jesus. Now when Jesus looked at him, He said, "You are Simon the son of Jonah. You shall be called Cephas" (which is translated, A Stone). (Jn. 1:41–42)

God sees beyond our present selves and sees His wonderful plan

The gospel includes the past salvation that took place through the cross.

But it also includes the future part of how God will continue to build us up

Philip is Called — Jn. 1:43–44

43The following day Jesus wanted to go to Galilee, and He found Philip and said to him, "Follow Me." 44Now Philip was from Bethsaida, the city of Andrew and Peter. (Jn. 1:43–44)

Jesus calls Philip who was from Bethsaida in Galilee
Philip Finds Nathanael — Jn. 1:45–46

45Philip found Nathanael and said to him, "We have found Him of whom Moses in the law, and also the prophets, wrote; Jesus of Nazareth, the son of Joseph." 46And Nathanael said to him, "Can anything good come out of Nazareth?" Philip said to him, "Come and see." (Jn. 1:45–46)

Moses' Prophecy — Deut. 18:15

15 The LORD your God will raise up for you a Prophet like me (Moses) from your midst, from your brethren. Him you shall hear, (Deut. 18:15)

Micah's Prophecy — Mic. 5:2

2"But you, Bethlehem Ephrathah, Though you are little among the thousands of Judah, Yet out of you shall come forth to Me The One to be Ruler in Israel, Whose goings forth are from of old, From everlasting." (Mic 5:2)

Jesus Who Knows Us #1 (Past) — Jn. 1:47–48

47Jesus saw Nathanael coming toward Him, and said of him, "Behold, an Israelite indeed, in whom is no deceit!" 48Nathanael said to Him, "How do You know me?" … (Jn. 1:47–48)

Jesus Who Knows Us #1 (Past): Jesus saw that Nathanael was someone who had lived uprightly before God
Jesus Who Knows Us #2 (Present) — Jn. 1:48–49

48… Jesus answered and said to him, "Before Philip called you, when you were under the fig tree, I saw you." 49Nathanael answered and said to Him, "Rabbi, You are the Son of God! You are the King of Israel! (ref. Ps. 2:6–8)" (Jn. 1:48–49)

Jesus Who Knows Us #2 (Present): Jesus knew the moment only God and himself knew
Jesus Who Knows Us #3 (Future) — Jn. 1:50–51

50Jesus answered and said to him, "Because I said to you, 'I saw you under the fig tree,' do you believe? You will see greater things than these. " 51And He said to him, "Most assuredly, I say to you, hereafter you shall see heaven open, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of Man. " (Jn. 1:50–51)

Jesus Who Knows Us #3 (Future): Jesus also prophesied that Nathanael would see heaven opened

Jesus knows our past, present, and future

We are able to live by entrusting our lives to Jesus
