Isaiah 52:13–15
1
Isaiah 52:13–14
13 Behold, My Servant shall deal prudently — He shall be exalted and extolled and be very high

The Messiah suffers before being exalted. God's plan includes both humiliation and ultimate glory for His Servant.

14 Just as many were astonished at you, so His visage was marred more than any man, and His form more than the sons of men.

The suffering of the Servant is extreme — His appearance was disfigured beyond recognition. This foreshadows the brutal suffering of Jesus at the cross.

2
Isaiah 52:15
15 So shall He sprinkle many nations.

Sprinkling of blood or oil for purification is mentioned throughout the Law of Moses (Lev 4:6; 8:11; 14:7). The Servant's work brings purification to all nations.

Kings shall shut their mouths at Him. For what had not been told them they shall see, and what they had not heard they shall consider.

Kings and nations will be stunned — surprised to see and hear things completely unexpected. The gospel of a suffering, risen King defies all human expectations.

Isaiah 53:1–11
3
Isaiah 53:1–5
1 Who has believed our report? And to whom has the arm of the Lord been revealed?

People will not believe the Messiah will come in a form of extreme humility to save them. The message of a suffering Savior is scandalous to human wisdom.

… 5 But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities. The chastisement for our peace was upon Him, and by His stripes we are healed.

He is pierced, wounded, and chastised in our place. Every wound He received was substitutionary — borne on our behalf so we could receive peace and healing.

4
Isaiah 53:6–9
6 All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned, everyone, to his own way.

Jesus died on the cross with criminals on both sides — He was counted among the transgressors (Isaiah 53:12), fulfilling prophecy precisely.

… 9 And they made His grave with the wicked, but with the rich at His death, because He had done no violence, nor was any deceit in His mouth.

He was placed in one of the tombs owned by Joseph of Arimathea — a wealthy man. A detail prophesied 700 years before Jesus' birth, fulfilled exactly.

5
Isaiah 53:10–11
10 … He has put Him to grief. When You make His soul an offering for sin, He shall see His seed, He shall prolong His days, and the pleasure of the Lord shall prosper in His hand.

He will enter the grave (v.9) — but then He will see His offspring and His days will be prolonged. This is Resurrection! Isaiah prophesied it centuries before it happened.

11 He shall see the labor of His soul, and be satisfied.

After resurrection, the Servant sees the fruit of His suffering and is satisfied. His death was not defeat — it was the path to ultimate victory and joy.

Ephesians · Romans
6
Ephesians 4:9–10
9 Now this, "He ascended" — what does it mean but that He also first descended into the lower parts of the earth? 10 He who descended is also the One who ascended far above all the heavens, that He might fill all things.

He resurrected and ascended to heaven and will carry out the plan to fill all things! His descent into death makes His ascension above all creation even more glorious.

7
Ephesians 1:9–10
9 … having made known to us the mystery of His will

Jesus, who is 100% God and 100% man, is the only King who will fully connect and rule both the realm of heaven and the realm of earth as one unified kingdom.

… 10 that in the dispensation of the fullness of the times He might gather together in one all things in Christ, both which are in heaven and which are on earth — in Him.

The ultimate goal of history: all things — heavenly and earthly — united under Christ. The resurrection is the turning point of this cosmic plan.

8
Ephesians 2:5–6
5 … even when we were dead in trespasses, made us alive together with Christ — by grace you have been saved — 6 and raised us up together, and made us sit together in the heavenly places in Christ Jesus.

Past tense — already accomplished! Even now we are those who are spiritually seated in heaven. He has overcome death, which we could never conquer, and He is inviting us into His victory!

9
Romans 6:5
5 For if we have been united together in the likeness of His death, certainly we also shall be in the likeness of His resurrection.

Through baptism, we are united with Christ. Our old self is crucified with Him, and through His resurrection we are raised to new life. Christ's resurrection brought a great spiritual transformation to the whole earth — beyond what we can fully understand.

10
Ephesians 3:9–10
9 … and to make all see what is the fellowship of the mystery … 10 to the intent that now the manifold wisdom of God might be made known by the church to the principalities and powers in the heavenly places.

The church was not established merely to barely win in spiritual battle — but to be those who make known the manifold wisdom of God to the spiritual powers themselves. We are God's display of wisdom to the universe.

God's call & our response
God is calling out to us through the resurrection of Jesus,

"I have an eternal kingdom prepared for you!
Come to Me and rise together with Me!"
And we declare,
"Let everything that was dead in me
come to life!"
