40 And the Child (Jesus) grew and became strong in spirit, filled with wisdom; and the grace of God was upon Him

Luke 2:40

This is the only record in the Bible of Jesus at the age of twelve, excluding His infancy, before He began His public ministry.

The Bible describes Jesus' childhood in three ways: 1) His spirit became strong, 2) He was filled with wisdom, and 3) the grace of God was upon Him.

80 So the child (John the Baptist) grew and became strong in spirit, and was in the deserts till the day of his manifestation to Israel

Luke 1:80

John the Baptist is also described in the record of his growth as having become strong in spirit.

Our spirit becomes strong through prayer, the Word, and worship.

2 The Spirit of the Lord shall rest upon Him, The Spirit of wisdom and understanding, The Spirit of counsel and might, The Spirit of knowledge and of the fear of the Lord

Isaiah 11:2

In the growth of Jesus, wisdom is especially emphasized — one of the seven spirits that come upon the Messiah.

52 And Jesus increased in wisdom and stature, and in favor with God and men

Luke 2:52

Wisdom is re-emphasized here as a central characteristic of Jesus' growth.

Jesus grew not only intellectually and spiritually, but also increased in favor — in relationship — with both God and people.

★ Prayer

"God, we pray that our next generation would grow as Jesus grew, that their spirit would become strong, that they would be filled with wisdom, that the grace of God would be upon them, and that they would grow in favor with both God and people."

41 His parents went to Jerusalem every year at the Feast of the Passover 42 And when He was twelve years old, they went up to Jerusalem according to the custom of the feast

Luke 2:41–42

Jewish men were required to visit Jerusalem three times a year: Passover, Pentecost, and the Feast of Tabernacles.

Jesus' entire family was a godly community that served God — His relatives also went up to Jerusalem together.

43 When they had finished the days, as they returned, the Boy Jesus lingered behind in Jerusalem. And Joseph and His mother did not know it 44 but supposing Him to have been in the company …

Luke 2:43–44

At that time, twelve-year-old Jesus stayed alone in the temple area for about three days during the spring weather of April.

44 … they went a day's journey, and sought Him among their relatives and acquaintances 45 So when they did not find Him, they returned to Jerusalem, seeking Him

Luke 2:44–45

A 12-year-old boy had disappeared from the family on a long trip — the urgency and anxiety of Mary and Joseph is palpable in this account.

46 Now so it was that after three days they found Him in the temple, sitting in the midst of the teachers, both listening to them and asking them questions 47 And all who heard Him were astonished at His understanding and answers

Luke 2:46–47

Sitting among the teachers was a recognized position of learning and dialogue in Jewish tradition.

Asking questions was an important way of passing down faith and knowledge for Jewish people.

The teachers — experts in the Torah — were astonished at the understanding and answers of this twelve-year-old boy.

48 So when they saw Him, they were amazed; and His mother said to Him, Son, why have You done this to us? Look, Your father and I have sought You anxiously

Luke 2:48

The Messiah — who would later calm the sea — being rebuked by His earthly parents. A profound picture of Jesus fully embracing the human experience of family and accountability.

Key Verse

49 And He said to them, Why did you seek Me? Did you not know that I must be about My Father's business? (NLT: Didn't you know that I must be in my Father's house?)

Luke 2:49

Jesus already recognized His identity as the Son of God the Father — at just twelve years old.

He lived His entire life making decisions rooted in that identity. This is the model for us as His disciples.

13 You are the salt of the earth; but if the salt loses its flavor, how shall it be seasoned? It is then good for nothing but to be thrown out and trampled underfoot by men. 14 You are the light of the world. A city that is set on a hill cannot be hidden

Matthew 5:13–14

Salt preserves and flavors — when we lose our spiritual identity, we lose our purpose and impact in the world.

When we live rooted in our spiritual identity and follow His teachings, we will stand as the light and the salt of this world.

23 At that time you won't need to ask me for anything. I tell you the truth, you will ask the Father directly, and he will grant your request because you use my name

John 16:23

When we don't know what to do as Jesus' disciples, we can seek guidance from the Holy Spirit directly!

15 I'm not asking you to take them out of the world, but to keep them safe from the evil one. 16 They do not belong to this world any more than I do. 17 Make them holy by your truth; teach them your word, which is truth

John 17:15–17

Jesus does not pray for us to escape the world, but to be protected and set apart within it — holy and purposeful.

Jesus lived a life rooted in His identity as the Son of God — and we will live a life as His disciples, rooted in ours.
